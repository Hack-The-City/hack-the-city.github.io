About

Who? 500 student hackers.

What? A hackathon.

Where? At New York University (NYU).

When? April 9-10, 2016 (tentative).

How? Magic.

Join 499 other students of all skill levels on NYU's campus to create something that you've never made before. We'll be feed you, teach you, and give you the resources that you need to create something great. Regardless of whether you're a designer, business person, coder, or some other kind of human, Hack The City will teach you something.

Your future questions, answered.

About Travel Hacking Other

What is Hack The City?

A student-organized hackathon, made with the goals of strengthening and advancing the hacker community.

What should I bring?

We suggest bringing a laptop, charger(s), your student ID, toiletries, a change of clothes, and a sleeping bag.

How do I get to NYU?

You can come by bus, train, plane, etc. We aren't doing travel reimbursements or sending out buses as of right now, but that may change.

Am I good enough to attend?

We encourage people of all levels to participate. There will be workshops targeted at anyone from beginners to the more experienced. HTC will be a place to learn new things, meet new people, and make mistakes.

Will food be provided?

Yes, there'll be lunch and dinner on Saturday, and breakfast and lunch on Sunday. There'll also be snacks throughout the hackathon.

How many people can be in each team?

Up to four people.

What if I don't have a team or idea?

It won't be an issue. We'll be holding an event to help you find team members, and you can brainstorm with other hackers.

What am I not allowed to bring?

Drugs, alcohol, weapons, etc.

Who can I contact with other questions that I have?

Send an email to VD Thatte at vdthatte@nyu.edu.
